Description

This interactive visualisation is showing the projects of 26 US Government Agencies. On the top you can see the projects' Lifecycle Cost ($M), Planned Cost ($M), Projected /Actual Cost ($M), Cost Variance ($M) and Schedule Variance (days) in parallel coordinates. Each of the projects is a polyline across the different axis. They are grouped by colour according to the agency name.

You can select portions of the data by dragging a rectangle or brushing over the axis. You will see the table below updating with data related to only your selection. Hovering over the table rows will highlight the paths on the graph on top. You can also sort the table based on the column values.

Questions to Answer

Brushing over Cost Variance allows us to see that Project 1770 from DoA has a 65 percent cost variance at $688.454

It is also clear that most projects' Schedule Variance was just over a year (in either way)

Brushing over Projected cost also tells us that DoA's Project 1657 is the most expensive project at $1.947B

Sorting the grid on Lifecycle Cost tells us that Department of State's Project 2998 costs $9.097B to keep alive


Controls

Drag and create rectangles on any number of axis (brushing) to select the data corresponding to that portion. Click on the same axis to remove selection

Drag the axis labels to drag the axis themselves.

Rows of the table will update based on your selection of the data on the top graph.

Hover over the rows to highlight the corresponding path.

Sort the table based on column values by clicking the column headers.

Go to different pages of the table by clicking page numbers.

This visualisation is responsive and will resize and reset when screen size changes

Intended Audience

The citizens of USA. They can use this visualisation to explore the dataset effectively, as well as get an overview at a project level to find patterns in the projects' cost and schedule.
