Description

This visualisation is a treemap and heatmap of projects of 26 US Government Agencies. Agencies have investments and investments have projects.

The heatmap is coloured based on the Cost Variance ($M) of the projects. Projects that saved or is projected to save more money than originally planned is greener and inversely projects that actually cost more are redder in the heatmap.

The treemap groups the projects into investments and, in turn, the investments into agencies. So, in the treemap, we can zoom into any agency to find its investments and then zoom into the investments to find the projects. At the same time, the aside panel will have detailed information about each cell of the treemap, be it a department, investment or a project. The ordering of the treemap is done by sorting on the projected/actual cost ($M)

Intended Audience

The citizens of USA. They can use this visualisation to view the US Agencies' spending on projects at a department and investment level. This also allows rich exploration of the underlying data in a hierarchical structure.

    Controls

    Click on any cell on the treemap to zoom in that section

    Click on the yellow bar to un-zoom back to previous section

    Scroll on the aside panel to get information about the cells in the treemap

    Click on the aside panel items to view them on the treemap

    The gradient legend is indication of the cell's Cost Variance ($M)

    Questions to Answer

    Using this visualisation, we can immediately see that Department of Labor's total Cost Variance ($M) was the most out of all 26 agencies

    At the same time, Department of Agriculture had the least Cost Variance ($M)

    We can also explore and find from the ordering of the treemap cells that National Archives and Records Administration spent only $21.24M in total, the least of all agencies.
